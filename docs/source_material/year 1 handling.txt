Future note: Year 1 handling

Current model:
Year 1 inputs manually seed the first projection row. This was a practical shortcut so the tool could avoid calculating a partial first year from today’s date.

Current Year 1 values may include:
- remaining spend for the first projection year
- known cash events, such as taxes, car purchase, wedding, or other one-time expenses
- manual brokerage / IRA / Roth draws so balances stay accurate
- planned Roth conversion
- MAGI-related values that still need better input structure

Important:
Do not treat year1_ as simply “current-year actuals.” Year 1 can include actuals, estimates, planned events, manual overrides, and partial-year assumptions.

Better long-term model:
Replace or reduce manual year1_ inputs by using:
- plan_start_date or today’s date
- annual spending target
- current account balances
- known one-time cash events
- known income / gains / losses
- planned Roth conversions
- draw order
- MAGI and ACA constraints

Then the engine can calculate the first partial year automatically:
- prorated Year 1 spending
- Year 1 cash needs
- Year 1 MAGI
- Year 1 withdrawals
- Year 1 ending balances

Design direction:
Keep year1_ inputs for now as a valid manual first-row seed/override system. Do not rename or redesign them until we intentionally decide whether Year 1 remains manual or becomes calculated from plan_start_date and annual assumptions.
